Planning first

Every long lasting product has a well-designed, solid plan behind it. Software are not exceptions. My workflow always starts with proper preparations and planning, resulting in:

  • Decision support documentations
  • Design documentations
  • System plans
  • Sequence diagrams
  • Class diagrams
  • Entity relationship diagrams
  • Control flow diagrams (if needed)

Spatially enabled

Based in science

What my research days really taught me is every complicated problem has a unique optimal solution. While optimal is usually unfeasible to pursue in business scenarios, it is always good to try and approximate with critical thinking and holistic evaluation. Computer science students usually ask me what good can a MSc in geography do to you; well, for example, the ability to think critically and evaluate problems with a holistic view.

  • It's no use to swing around with patterns and methods just to look smart (even with Scrum... especially with Scrum). Always understand the problem and its context first.
  • Modern problems are usually too complicated for pure solutions. Don't be afraid to apply hybrid ones even by mixing patterns from different disciplines.
  • Always try to look at the full picture. Sometimes it's better to start with a less complicated stack than making a fresh team choke on quality.
