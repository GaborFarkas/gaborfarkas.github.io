Based in science

What my days in research taught me is every complicated problem has a unique optimal solution. While optimal is usually unfeasible to pursue in business scenarios, it is always good to try and approximate it with critical thinking and holistic evaluation. Computer science students sometimes ask me: what good can an MSc in geography do for you? Well, for example, the ability to think critically and evaluate problems from a holistic view.

  • It's no use to swing around with patterns and methods to look smart (even with Scrum... especially Scrum). Always understand the problem and its context first.
  • Modern problems are usually too complicated for pure solutions. Don't be afraid to apply hybrid ones, even by mixing patterns from different disciplines.
  • Always try to look at the complete picture. Sometimes, it is better to start with a less complicated stack than making a fresh team choke on quality.
