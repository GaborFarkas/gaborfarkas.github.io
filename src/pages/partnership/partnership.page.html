With hard work, after countless restless hours, you built a company. You have the fundamental building blocks in place. You have working management, a network of possible partners, and sources for funding. What next?

If you happen to find yourself in a situation where you would like to extend your company with an IT-focused branch (e.g. a SaaS product), but you don't know how, I can lend you my experience. Let's be realistic: this is a niche service with few opportunities, but this is where I value myself the best. I can assemble the pieces for a basic scalable service portfolio, which can be extended dynamically by either your team or any software development company. This is not a software development counseling service. This is a complete package of software architecture, development, infrastructure, building a team of developers, mentoring, and project management techniques.

How it works?

We have some initial meetings, where you introduce me to your goals, current plans, your overall situation.

During this phase, we will create a working frame together. We need to establish what you plan to achieve, the key points in your company's life, and where you need my help. We will estimate a realistic time frame, and a starting software complexity, then plan an organic growth track.

We will talk about topics like:

  • An approximate time frame
  • System complexity
  • Team structure, if we should build a team
  • Key competencies needed for further development and maintenance

We make every major decision on the stack, and I create all the initial system overview plans.

I start this phase by preparing decision support documents, where necessary. You should be able to correctly assess the ramifications of choosing a technology stack over a different one and make the right decisions. After we settle on the languages, the frameworks, and the architecture, I create the initial overview plan documentation using C4.

We decide on the collaborative platforms we would like to use for sharing plans and other software documentation in this phase as well. This system can be anything from a well-established cloud provider to a locally hosted version control system. It will most likely change further into the scaling process.

I develop the system-critical custom components.

Reinventing the wheel is bad for business. During the planning phase, we specify components we should take off the shelf as a paid service or an open-source production-ready solution. We also identify every component that should be hand-crafted for your system. From these components, system-critical ones are developed by me, to ensure a quality, consistent, maintainable backbone.

Parallel to these developments, we start outlining the infrastructure for the production version of the system. We start using version control, CI/CD, and agile project management methods. This is also a great starting point for building up a team of developers through less critical and complicated custom components.

We evaluate the complexity of the overall system together according to the planned growth track. If you would like to build a team of junior and medior developers, we start with less complexity and help them into your company's life step by step through mentoring. If you would outsource maintenance and further development to a professional company, we start with a sophisticated and more complex stack.

We set up every cloud service needed to run the system and support future development.

Stable and scalable production systems are not the privilege of large multinational IT companies, although they need experience, knowledge, and much care to work correctly. An efficient infrastructure is essential for any production system, so the next step is to plan, install, and configure the necessary software and write some automatization workflows.

In this phase, we set up the required servers for the system to work. We make decisions on the granularity of the system, like how many stages the software should have (e.g. nightly, staging, production). I can configure some Linux servers (if some components do not require otherwise) with the necessary software, workflows, and automatization scripts.

While every step of my service includes a strong DevSecOps line, I'm not afraid to tell you, that infrastructure is not my strongest suit. If your system needs much more than simple containerization, Linux servers, and bash scripts, this is the point where we need to look for a DevOps expert to complement my skills.

I closely monitor your progress but give you gradually more space to make technical decisions without my advice.

Albeit the final, this is the lengthiest phase of our collaboration. At this point, every cornerstone of the system is set and working, and we are only polishing and extending it. Judging from your technical decisions and assessments, I keep some distance from steering and management duties and concentrate on tasks you deem more important for your business.

Software development gets more concentrated from this point. I fix all the bugs we can find in custom-written components, and we gradually introduce the future maintainers and developers of the system to all parts. Mentoring continues for junior and medior colleagues if required.

The end of this phase, coincident with the end of our long-term partnership, is agreed on mutually but mainly defined by you. When you feel confident in the future of your system, your technical preparedness, and your team, you are free and welcome to say goodbye and reduce our collaboration to occasional consulting, if required.

Accountability

It is not only normal but logical to have some questions about the legal and financial aspects of this service. Before anything else, long-term partnerships are based on mutual trust. I'm only comfortable starting them when we have known each other for some time and figured out that we can work together efficiently, and have common goals and similar mindsets. If you are considering this package, please start with small-scale projects or counsels to confirm if we can work together for years.

Before getting into the planning phase, we sign the necessary contracts. As I don't have special legal obligations to any third party, it is your task to phrase the initial framework contract. Please keep in mind that I normally don't sign anything limiting my freedom, such as exclusivity contracts. On the other hand, I'm happy to sign any non-disclosure agreement. I'm also open to helping you phrase certificates of completion or other documentation you or your company needs.

My long-term partners get my best rates. However, they are hourly. In long-term partnerships, I invoice monthly according to the hours dedicated to you in the previous month. You can track every detail of the completed work through Clockify shared reports anytime. The monthly time budget is agreed on previously, so you can easily plan with my costs. Since long-term partnerships are unique, prices are calculated and tailored for each of them exclusively.
